Context:

The real estate markets, like those in Sydney and Melbourne, present an interesting opportunity for data analysts to analyze and predict where property prices are moving towards. Prediction of property prices is becoming increasingly important and beneficial. Property prices are a good indicator of both the overall market condition and the economic health of a country. Considering the data provided, we are wrangling a large set of property sales records stored in an unknown format and with unknown data quality issues

Dataset features:

date: The date the property was listed or sold.

price: The price of the property.

bedrooms: The number of bedrooms in the house.

bathrooms: The number of bathrooms.

sqft_living: The total square footage of the interior living space.

sqft_lot: The total square footage of the land lot.

floors: The number of floors in the house.

waterfront: A binary indicator (likely 0 or 1) of whether the property has a waterfront view.

view: A rating for the quality of the property's view.

condition: A rating representing the overall condition of the house.

sqft_above: The square footage of the house located above ground level.

sqft_basement: The square footage of the basement.

yr_built: The year the house was originally built.

yr_renovated: The year the house was last renovated (listed as 0 if never renovated).

street: The street address of the property.

city: The city where the property is located.

statezip: The state and ZIP code of the property.

country: The country where the property is located.
